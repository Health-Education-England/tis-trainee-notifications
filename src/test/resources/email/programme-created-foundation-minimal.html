Pre-employment checks and processes

Both NHS England and your employing organisation understand that you will be busy in this transition period to the start of your upcoming training programme, but we wish to emphasise the importance of engaging with all organisations with the onboarding process.

Your employing organisation will be contacting you about pre-employment checks from 12 weeks prior to starting (if not sooner). It is essential you avoid delaying these processes so as not to cause any disruption to your employment or training programme. The Medical Staffing teams (or Lead employers) will be processing a large number of new starters to their organisation and so any delay may impact their ability to add you to their employment systems, such as payroll.

Please ensure you contact your employing organisation if you have not heard anything by 8 weeks prior to your start date. Your employing organisation's details will be contained in the placement confirmation email that you will receive prior to starting.

This timescale is in line with the Code of Practice agreed in conjunction with the BMA and NHS Employers.

Please be aware allocations made to placements after 12 weeks will impact on the timelines stated above.

Their pre-employment checks will include:

  • Your right to work
  • If you have a criminal record
  • Employment history and references
  • Professional Registration and qualifications
  • Fitness to work

Changing Hours (LTFT Applications)

To meet national guidance, all Changing hours (LTFT) applications must be submitted a minimum of 16 weeks before your LTFT start date or before the next rotation change dependent on local NHS England office processes.

This allows Trusts the required 12 weeks’ notice under the Code of Practice, giving them time to make rota changes, update work schedules, and ensure your placement is appropriately planned.
